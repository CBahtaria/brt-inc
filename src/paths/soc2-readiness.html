SOC 2 Readiness for Startups

A plain-language guide to what it involves, what it costs, and whether you need it yet.

What is SOC 2, actually?

SOC 2 proves to customers and enterprise procurement teams that you handle their data securely. An independent auditor examines your systems and controls, then issues a formal report. Two types exist:

Type I — Snapshot

Auditor looks at your controls as they exist on a specific date and confirms they're designed correctly.

Timeline: 2–3 months

Good for: "We need something now."

Type II — Operating effectiveness

Auditor observes your controls operating over 3–6 months, then spends another month on the audit itself.

Timeline: 4–7 months total

Good for: Enterprise sales, procurement departments.

Most startups do Type I first, then Type II on the next cycle.

The process, step by step

  1. Week 1–2: Scoping & gap analysis

    Determine which Trust Services Criteria apply to your product (Security is mandatory; Availability, Confidentiality, Privacy, and Processing Integrity are optional). Define which systems are in scope. Document what you already have. Document what's missing. You receive a scoping document and gap analysis you can take to any auditor — it's yours regardless of who does the audit.

  2. Week 3–8: Implementation

    Close the gaps. This typically covers: MDM deployment, written security policies (we have templates), MFA across all critical systems, vulnerability scanning setup, logging and monitoring, a penetration test, and a team security training session (30 minutes, we run it). We write the configs — Terraform, Python, bash — not just a checklist.

  3. Audit readiness review

    Before the auditor starts, we check every control against what they'll ask for, organise the evidence package, and sit in on the audit kickoff call to make sure your team knows what to expect.

  4. Audit period

    The auditor leads this phase. We're available for technical clarification and to answer auditor questions on your behalf. The SOC 2 report arrives 2–4 weeks after the audit observation period closes.

Who picks the auditor? You do. We'll recommend 2–3 startup-friendly firms based on your budget and timeline. You contract directly with them — we don't take referral fees.

Do we even need this?

Honest answer: maybe not yet.

  • If one enterprise customer would 3x your revenue and SOC 2 is blocking the deal — yes, do it now.
  • If someone told you "it's good to have" — save the money. Do a security baseline for $8k first. Revisit when customers are actively asking for it.

We'll tell you if you're jumping the gun.
